Presentation

Marsh ecosystems are particularly sensitive to climate change. Species distribution are indeed strongly dependent on the extent of high tides and on their interactions with other species. In coastal habitats, one manifestation of climate change is accelerating sea level rise, this long-term slow rise being hidden within much greater annual variation. In the Long Term Ecological Research Site “Georgia Coastal Ecosystems”, long-term observation are made in order to analyze the impact of climate change on this ecosystem functioning. This game will help thanks to you to collect useful data for studying this impact.

(more details)

More precisely, we set up an annual survey based on images taken rigorously on the same site every year since 2010. Together with the climatic and soil data collected on the site, this survey will help to analyse the ecological processes linked with species distribution and their interactions (for instance trophic interactions). Thanks to the game, we will get a huge amount of ecological data of interests in order to document species distribution and investigate with an unprecedented accuracy the mechanisms underlying their spatial patterns.
